# D17 Рђћ The invariant-detector admission law (what survives?)

Status: **META-CONTRACT.** D17 does not define a subsystem. It names the *admission law* that
every gated detector in this project has already been following, and makes that law explicit and
falsifiable. Its correctness test is **retro-admission**: the existing detectors (D14, D15, D16,
rigidity, criticality) must each satisfy it Рђћ *if even one cannot be expressed under D17, D17 is
wrong.* That test was run against the committed gate (`e390f42`) and all five pass; the mapping is
┬Д6. This is an unusually strong specification because it is falsifiable by code that already exists.

The organizing question is not a prover's ("is proposition P true?") nor a CAS's ("compute f(x)?")
but:

> **Which exact statements about an object remain true under a declared class of transformations?**

An invariant is a value that is *constant on the orbits of that class*. A detector computes one, on
a domain where it can, and **refuses** everywhere else.

## 1. The detector

A detector is a tuple

    ­ЮњЪ = (Dom, Inv, W, ~, R)

- **Dom** Рђћ a **decidable** domain. Membership is checkable.
- **Inv : Dom Рєњ V** Рђћ an **exact** invariant map (no rounding, or bounded-regime honesty).
- **W** Рђћ an **independently checkable witness** that recomputes / certifies the value.
- **~** Рђћ a **declared equivalence relation** on Dom, generated by a declared class of *admissible
  transformations* (morphisms). It need NOT be a group action: a group (invertible) is the special
  case; monoids (non-invertible evolution, presentation edits) and bare equivalences (provenance,
  partition choices) are the general case. `Inv` is invariant iff it **factors through the quotient
  `Dom/~`** Рђћ i.e. `Inv(x) = Inv(y)` whenever `x ~ y`.
- **R** Рђћ a **total refusal relation** outside Dom: every object not in Dom gets a *typed* refusal,
  never a wrong answer.

## 2. The admission law

A detector is **admitted** iff:

1. **Membership is decidable** Рђћ `x Рѕѕ Dom?` is computable.
2. **REFUSE is total outside the domain** Рђћ `x РѕЅ Dom РЄњ R` fires, typed.
3. **The invariant is exactly computable** Рђћ `Inv` is deterministic and exact.
4. **The witness recomputes the invariant** Рђћ (4a) self-verifies the value; and, for the *cross-placed*
   grade, (4b) an independent placement reproduces it (the D8 move).
5. **The invariant is constant on a declared corpus of `~`-transformations** Рђћ `Inv(T┬иx) = Inv(x)`.
6. **A deliberately non-invariant quantity reddens the gate** Рђћ a `Q` with `~ Ріё ker(Q)` must fail,
   proving the invariance is load-bearing and not vacuous.

Nothing more, nothing less Рђћ and nowhere does it mention topology, physics, graphics, or
networking. That silence is why it generalizes.

**Every theorem ships with its counterexample.** Conditions 2 and 6 are first-class, not
afterthoughts: admission is measured as a **positive corpus + a negative corpus + typed refusals**,
which is precisely what a well-formed gate stage in this repo already records Рђћ
`golden` ┬и `invariance property` ┬и `-selftest` (non-invariance) ┬и `-refusal`.

## 3. Two relations, and completeness as a graded claim

A detector carries two relations: the **declared** `~` (what it must identify) and the **induced
kernel** `ker(Inv) = {(x,y) : Inv(x) = Inv(y)}` (what it actually identifies). Admission requires
`~ Ріє ker(Inv)`.

The detector is **complete** iff `ker(Inv) = ~` Рђћ the invariant separates *exactly* the orbits, so
`Inv(x) = Inv(y) РЪ║ x ~ y`. Completeness is a **separate** claim from admission and is frequently
undecidable (deciding orbit-equality can be harder than computing the invariant). It is therefore
**graded** like everything else Рђћ `PARTIAL`, `MEASURED-complete` (on a corpus), or `PROVEN-complete`
Рђћ and never a free label. Complete detectors are terminal for their `~`: every weaker detector on
the same domain **factors through** them.

## 3a. Maturity Рђћ two orthogonal axes (reproduction ├Ќ separation)

A detector's maturity is **two independent axes**, not one ladder; conflating them overstates what a
detector guarantees.

- **Axis A Рђћ reproduction (witness independence):** `REFERENCE` (condition 4a Рђћ the witness self-verifies
  the value on the corpus) Рєњ `CROSS-PLACED` (4b Рђћ an *independent* implementation reproduces it
  byte-for-byte, the D8 move) Рєњ *frozen* (cross-placed + an immutable surface, D12).
- **Axis B Рђћ separation (completeness):** `PARTIAL` (`~ Ріє ker(Inv)` Рђћ sound: never distinguishes
  equivalent objects, but may merge inequivalent ones) Рєњ `COMPLETE` (`ker(Inv) = ~` Рђћ separates exactly;
  graded measured-vs-proven, often undecidable).

The axes are **orthogonal**: a cross-placed detector can be partial (criticality's conserved total is
cross-placeable yet does not separate transport orbits), and a reference detector can be complete without a
second placement. Keeping Axis B separate is what stops "exact" or "cross-placed" from silently implying
"complete." Completeness is not a blanket default in either direction Рђћ it is claimed only where justified,
and *withheld* where it does not hold. The current maturity map (the embryonic detector atlas):

| Detector | A ┬и reproduction | B ┬и separation |
|---|---|---|
| **D14** canon | cross-placed | **COMPLETE** by construction Рђћ a canonical form is the complete invariant of the equivalence it canonicalizes |
| **D15** view | cross-placed | **COMPLETE** by construction Рђћ the invariant *is* authoritative identity (modulo digest collision-resistance) |
| **D16** region | cross-placed ┬и frozen | **SCOPED-complete** Рђћ complete for the *declared* static-partition `~` by construction (modulo digest collision-resistance); the `~` is itself narrow, and completeness for a broader (dynamic-repartition) `~` would require a theorem, not corpus evidence |
| **rigidity** | cross-placed | **PARTIAL** Рђћ the verdict does not determine a framework up to congruence |
| **criticality** | reference | **PARTIAL** Рђћ many transport histories share one conserved total |
| **toric** `k` | cross-placed (C99 self-verified; Rust for Windows) | **COMPLETE** for surface homeomorphism (genus classifies closed orientable surfaces); deliberately *not* a complete *code* invariant (distance is separate, NP-hard) |
| **persim** barcode | reference | **PARTIAL** Рђћ distinct filtered complexes can share a barcode; the barcode does not recover the complex |
| **winding** `w` | reference | **PARTIAL** Рђћ many curves share a winding number about a probe; `w` does not recover the curve (reversal covariance `w Рєњ Рѕњw` documented, not in `~`) |

The toric row is the discipline in miniature: complete with respect to its own invariant, refusing to claim
more than it computes. As the catalog grows past ~20 detectors this table becomes the **map of the project**
Рђћ the eight-column form `(Domain, Invariant, Witness, Relation, Coverage, Reproduction, Separation)` is the
natural expansion.

## 4. The lattice of detectors

Order detectors by their induced kernel: a finer kernel distinguishes more. If `~РѓЂ Ріє ~Рѓѓ` then
`InvРѓѓ` is *coarser* (`InvРѓѓ(x)=InvРѓѓ(y)` is a weaker statement Рђћ more is identified). This is a genuine
partial order: some detectors distinguish more, some deliberately forget more, and complete
invariants sit at the bottom of their domain's order. Organizing the library *is* building this
lattice.

## 5. The invariant profile (the API)

An object does not have *an* invariant; it has a **profile** Рђћ the table

    { detector Рєњ value | REFUSE }

Each detector answers where it is competent and refuses where it is not. Example (illustrative):

    URDROBJ2 object
      canonical (D14):   digest 3c36067Рђд
      rigidity:          RIGID (dof 0)
      topology (HРѓЂ):     REFUSE  (not a chain complex)
      criticality:       REFUSE  (not a population field)
      symmetry:          REFUSE  (no detector admitted yet)

Nothing speculative, nothing inferred Рђћ exact detectors answering only where competent. This is the
honest form of "map the invariant landscape of an object": **not** universal introspection (which is
undecidable Рђћ no algorithm returns the symmetry group or conserved quantities of an *arbitrary*
object), but a **finite library of detectors with decidable domains and total REFUSE**, whose union
of non-refusing answers is the profile. REFUSE does the same work here that `FIELD-REFUSE` and
`WORLD-REFUSE` do everywhere else: it lets the engine say *"I can't tell, from here,"* which is what
keeps *"what survives?"* honest.

## 7. Self-application

**"Evidence Against C8" (D5) is D17 applied to the engine's own primitive set.** The object is the
sealed alphabet; the admissible transformation is *"admit a new domain"*; the invariant under test
is *"the primitive set is unchanged"*; the ledger is the corpus of orbits checked. The engine
already runs its central question on itself Рђћ and every clean composition is one more orbit on which
the alphabet proved invariant.

## 9. Coverage Рђћ the boundary of what is claimed exact

The library's value is not only what it computes but *where it stops*. Each admitted invariant carries an
explicit exactness boundary, and as the catalog grows the interesting questions become ones of **coverage**,
not implementation: *which mathematical domains admit exact, witnessed detectors; which require
approximation; which are provably undecidable or intractable on general inputs and therefore must carry a
narrow domain or a typed refusal.* Every detector falls into one of:

- **Exact on all of `Dom`** Рђћ computed exactly for every admitted object (toric `k = dim HРѓЂ`; the rigidity
  verdict; conservation totals; the witness digests).
- **Exact only on a narrow domain because the general problem is hard** Рђћ admitted on a structured
  sub-family and `REFUSE`d elsewhere rather than approximated (toric *distance*: exact `= L` for the L├ЌL
  torus, `TORIC-REFUSE` in general Рђћ minimum distance is NP-hard).
- **Bounded regime** Рђћ exact until a declared bound, then a typed refusal, never a wrap (criticality
  `FIELD-REFUSE`; rigidity overflow `REFUSE`; the Q32.32 substrate).
- **Refuse-general / undecidable** Рђћ no exact detector exists on arbitrary input; admitted only on a
  decidable sub-domain, everything else refused.

A catalog organized this way documents not just what Ur├░r computes, but the precise frontier of what it
claims to compute *exactly* Рђћ the honest complement to *"what survives?"*: **where can we still answer at
all?**
